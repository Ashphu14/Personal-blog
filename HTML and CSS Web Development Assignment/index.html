Rajastan's Dal Baati Churma

Discover the vibrant flavors of Rajastan Traditional food scene

'Dal Baati Churma' is a traditional delicacy from the state of Rajasthan. It is associated with the festivals of Makar Sankranti and Diwali in the Dhundhand region. It is also prepared on special occasions like marriage ceremonies and housewarming. Dal Bati Churma is a complete & very popular dish in Rajasthan. It is a combination of three different food items - Dal (lentils), Bati & Churma (Sweet). Dal Baati is comprising Dal (lentils) and Baati (hard wheat rolls). It is popular in other parts of Madhya Pradesh also. Dal is prepared using Tuvaar Dal, Chana Dal (prepared by removing the skin of split chickpeas), Mung dal. The pulses or lentils are cooked together after being soaked in water for a few hours.

"Spicy, hearty, rustic, Rajasthani delight."

Gujarat's Fafda Jalebi

Discover the vibrant flavors of Gujarat's "ફાફડા જલેબી"

Jalebi Fafda is a popular Indian snack that is native to the western state of Gujarat. It is a combination of two separate snacks, Jalebi and Fafda. Jalebi is a sweet, deep-fried snack made from a batter of all-purpose flour, yogurt, and sugar. The batter is spiraled into hot oil and then deep-fried until golden brown. Jalebi is then soaked in sugar syrup until saturated. Fafda is a savory, deep-fried snack made from a batter of chickpea flour, spices, and baking soda. The batter is rolled out into thin sheets and then cut into strips. The strips are then deep-fried until golden brown. Fafda is typically served with chutney on the side. Jalebi Fafda is a popular snack for breakfast, lunch, or dinner. It is also a popular snack to serve at festivals and parties. Jalebi Fafda is a delicious and satisfying snack that is sure to please everyone who tries it.

"Savory meets sweet in Gujarat."
